# E-E-A-T Assessment Reference

Google's quality rater guidelines evaluate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Use this checklist during Phase 6 of the audit.

## Experience
Signals that the author/site has first-hand experience with the topic.
- [ ] Original photos, screenshots, or videos (not stock)
- [ ] Personal anecdotes or case study results
- [ ] Before/after comparisons with real data
- [ ] Review or testing language ("I tried", "we tested", "in our experience")
- [ ] User-generated content: reviews, comments, community contributions

## Expertise
Signals that the content creator has domain knowledge.
- [ ] Author name and bio visible on the page
- [ ] Author credentials relevant to the topic (e.g., MD for medical, CPA for finance)
- [ ] Author has a dedicated author page with credential detail
- [ ] Accurate, specific, up-to-date information (no vague generalities)
- [ ] Technical depth appropriate to the topic
- [ ] Citations and links to primary sources (studies, official docs)

## Authoritativeness
Signals that the site/author is recognized as a leading source.
- [ ] Backlinks from authoritative sites in the niche
- [ ] Media mentions or press coverage visible
- [ ] Industry awards, certifications, or memberships mentioned
- [ ] Referenced/cited by other reputable sources
- [ ] Social proof: follower counts, reviews, testimonials from credible sources

## Trustworthiness
Signals that the site is honest, transparent, and safe.
- [ ] HTTPS across entire site (not just homepage)
- [ ] Privacy policy linked in footer
- [ ] Terms of service / legal pages accessible
- [ ] Clear contact information (address, phone, email)
- [ ] No deceptive advertising (ads clearly labeled)
- [ ] Transparent about affiliations, sponsorships, or conflicts of interest
- [ ] Return policy / refund policy for e-commerce
- [ ] Real customer reviews (not just curated testimonials)
- [ ] No broken links or 404 errors on key trust pages
- [ ] Content regularly updated (freshness signals)

## YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) — Extra Scrutiny

Apply heightened E-E-A-T standards to any page touching:
- Health / medical advice → requires licensed professional authorship
- Financial planning / investment → requires certified financial credentials
- Legal advice → requires licensed attorney review
- News / current events → requires editorial standards disclosure
- Safety information → requires authoritative sourcing

**YMYL Red Flags:**
- Health advice with no medical author or review disclosure
- Financial recommendations with no credentials or disclaimer
- Legal content with no attorney review
- Undisclosed affiliate content that influences purchase decisions
- No "last reviewed" date on health/financial content
